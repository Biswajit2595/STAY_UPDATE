Google launches 'passkeys' for password-free sign-in

Google has begun rolling out 'passkeys' that lets users sign into their Google accounts without a password or 2-Step Verification. Passkeys enable users to securely sign in to their Google accounts using their fingerprint, face, screen lock, or hardware security key. \"Unlike passwords, passkeys can only exist on your devices,\" Google said in a blog post.

How does Google's password-free sign-in 'passkey' work?

'Passkeys' let users sign in to their Google Account with fingerprint, face scan, or device screen lock, like a PIN. Unlike passwords, passkeys can only exist on your devices. \"The main ingredient of passkeys is cryptographic private key – this is what is stored on your devices...When you create one, the corresponding public key is uploaded to Google,\" said Google.

22-year-old Haaland breaks record for scoring most goals in a single PL season

Manchester City's 22-year-old forward Erling Haaland broke the record for scoring most goals in a single Premier League season on Wednesday night. Haaland took his tally of goals in Premier League 2022-23 to 35 with a 70th-minute goal against West Ham United. Andy Cole (Newcastle United, 1993-94) and Alan Shearer (Blackburn Rovers 1994-95) jointly held the previous record (34 goals).
